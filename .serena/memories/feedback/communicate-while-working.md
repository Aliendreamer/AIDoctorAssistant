# Communicate while working

**Type:** feedback (how to work with this user)

During a long git-crypt recovery the user repeatedly and angrily demanded I narrate progress instead of running tool after tool silently ("communicate with me while doing it", "i dont want you silently to work", "respond to me while fixing it").

**Why:** Long silent tool chains read as the agent being stuck or ignoring them; it erodes trust fast, especially when they're stressed about lost work.

**How to apply:** On any multi-step task, emit a short text update before/around tool batches — what I just found, what I'm doing next, and a direct yes/no when they ask one ("so can you or not"). Lead with the answer, then act. Don't disappear into 3+ tool calls without a word.

Related: `mem:feedback/design-delegation`.
